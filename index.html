About me

I'm a PhD student at Imperial College London, guided by Tolga Birdal. I am passionate about AI research that benefits humanity. My current research focuses on 3D vision and robotics, with broader interests in generative models, video understanding, and world models. I am a big fan of Yoshua Bengio.

Previously, I worked as a Machine Learning Engineer at ByteDance, where I optimized user experience and developed models that significantly reduced customer complaint rates, impacting hundreds of millions of users.

Before that, I was a research intern at INSAIT, working on video generation with Luc Van Gool and Danda Paudel. I received my Master's degree from Sun Yat-sen University, where I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Wei-Shi Zheng on video understanding research. I also interned at Intelligent Driving Group (IDG) of Baidu, and NetEase Games AI Lab.

Outside of work, I love hiking and mountaineering. I enjoy bouldering and the gym. I am also an activist committed to protecting our planet from global warming.
